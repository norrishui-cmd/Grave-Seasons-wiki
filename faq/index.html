Guide FAQ

How does the Grave Seasons journal record clues?

Discovered clues are stored in the journal so players can review what they found during an investigation instead of memorizing every crime-scene detail.

Do clues become investigation quests in Grave Seasons?

Yes. Confirmed journal clues can become trackable investigation quests that point the player toward another person, place, or connection to examine.

Do resident routines matter when following clues?

Yes. The developer's journal example shows that knowing a resident's usual locations can help when a clue points to them but their workplace is closed.

Does the journal solve the murder case for you?

No. The journal tracks evidence and objectives, but the player still has to connect clues with Ashenridge residents, businesses, relationships, and locations.

Are home break-ins required for every killer campaign?

Breaking into homes is a confirmed investigation option, but no official source says that every campaign requires a break-in to reach its ending.

Can a Grave Seasons guide stay spoiler-free?

Yes. System explanations, preparation advice, journal use, and location context can be presented without naming the active killer, victim, or ending.

Will each killer need a different walkthrough?

Yes. Every potential killer has an authored campaign with individual reasoning, kill style, and targets, so one fixed case route cannot cover all playthroughs.

Gameplay FAQ

Are crops seasonal in Grave Seasons?

Yes. Official descriptions confirm seasonal crops, and changing seasons affect the world around Ashenridge.

Does Grave Seasons have different farm plot types?

Yes. Steam states that seasonal crops can be grown using different types of farm plots.

Can farming reveal Ashenridge history?

Yes. Official copy says players can unearth pieces of Ashenridge history while farming, harvesting, and foraging.

Can you customize the farm?

Yes. Potential aesthetic customization options for the farm are confirmed alongside tool and building upgrades.

Can you stay awake for more than one in-game day?

Yes. Steam advertises staying awake for more than 24 hours and experiencing both day and night in Ashenridge.

Can choices have both positive and negative consequences?

Yes. Official descriptions explicitly say decisions can produce positive and negative consequences during the player's year.

Does the story continue after a resident dies?

Yes. Character death unlocks some stories, while survival can reveal different storylines; a death is not automatically a game over.

Character FAQ

Do resident quests help build trust?

Yes. Official feature descriptions connect completing quests for townsfolk with earning their trust and learning more about them.

Can a romance candidate become the killer?

Yes. Steam confirms that some of the 12 potential romance options can be selected as the killer in a playthrough.

Can a romance candidate become a victim?

Yes. Official descriptions allow townsfolk to be victims while relationships and survival outcomes continue to shape the story.

Can the same character have different story roles?

Yes. A resident can have different relevance when they are alive, dead, a victim, or the active killer in separate playthroughs.

Does the surviving resident roster change Ashenridge?

Yes. Steam says Ashenridge changes depending on which residents are alive, along with the active killer and current season.

Do character jobs help with investigations?

Yes. The journal example shows that a business-related clue can require knowing which resident works there and where they spend time.

Are all Ashenridge residents romanceable?

No. The game has more than 30 residents but only 12 potential romance options.

Location FAQ

What are the seven major areas in Grave Seasons?

The seven named areas are the Farm, Mountainside, Forest, Local District, Commercial District, Coast and Docks, and Mines.

Does Ashenridge really have more than 70 interiors?

Yes. The official Steam description states that Ashenridge contains more than 70 interior locations.

Is the player's farm one of the major areas?

Yes. Your Farm is explicitly included in the official list of seven major Ashenridge areas.

Where is the player farm located?

Official copy places the farm at the very top of Ashenridge's mountain.

Are the Coast and Docks one major area?

Yes. “The Coast & Docks” appears as one combined entry in the official seven-area list.

Are the Mines a major Ashenridge area?

Yes. The Mines are one of the seven official major locations and mining is a confirmed activity.

Can locations change between playthroughs?

Yes. Ashenridge can change with the active killer, resident survival, and season.

Item FAQ

What does the 600-plus item count include?

Steam says players can collect, discover, or craft more than 600 items, but it does not publish a category breakdown.

How are items obtained in Grave Seasons?

Official copy identifies three broad methods: collecting, discovering, and crafting more than 600 items.

Can occult items save residents?

Some mysterious crafted items can help protect potential victims from supernatural killers.

Where are recipes crafted?

Official descriptions name two farm crafting locations: the kitchen and the shed.

Can tools be upgraded in Grave Seasons?

Yes. Tool upgrades are an officially confirmed part of farm progression.

Can farm buildings be upgraded?

Yes. Steam confirms upgrades for buildings on the player's farm.

Can fishing and mining ingredients help solve mysteries?

Yes. Official copy connects new ingredients found through fishing and mining with unraveling Ashenridge's mysteries.
