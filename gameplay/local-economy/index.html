Grave Seasons Local Economy and Selling Goods

Grave Seasons has a local economy where players sell produced and gathered goods in town. Harvests also support investigations, so the best item may not always be the one with the highest sale value.

Confirmed data

  • Selling goods in town: confirmed
  • Farm produce: sellable category
  • Fishing and mining: economy inputs
  • Mystery use can compete with sale use
  • Prices and shop hours: not published

Confirmed economy loop

Official descriptions connect growing crops, fishing, mining, and participating in Ashenridge’s economy by selling goods. Revealed resident jobs also place shops and services throughout town, making production part of the social world rather than an isolated farm menu.

Why resources should not be ranked only by profit

Harvested and discovered ingredients can help unravel mysteries or support protective crafting. A crop, fish, mineral, or forage item may therefore have a recipe, quest, clue, relationship, or victim-protection use that is more important than its sale price.

Future table fields

Post-launch economy tables will record buy and sell values, shop, opening times, season, acquisition cost, processing path, recipe use, and investigation relevance. No profitability ranking will be published before those values are tested.

Source and verification boundary

This page uses the official Steam game description. Counts and announced functions are reported as confirmed; unpublished names, prices, schedules, recipes, outcomes, and balance values are not inferred.
